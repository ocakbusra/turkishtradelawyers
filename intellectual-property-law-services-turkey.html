Anti-Counterfeiting & Enforcement

Vigorous defense against infringement and illicit activities.

Intellectual property (IP) is a critical asset for businesses operating in today's knowledge-based economy. Protecting these assets requires a comprehensive and strategic approach that encompasses not only registration and maintenance but also proactive monitoring, vigorous enforcement, and strategic commercialization. The Turkish legal framework for intellectual property is primarily governed by the Industrial Property Code (Law No. 6769), which consolidated and modernized the regulations concerning trademarks, patents, designs, and geographical indications in alignment with European Union standards and international treaties such as the Paris Convention, the TRIPS Agreement, and the Madrid Protocol.

Navigating this complex regulatory environment demands specialized expertise and a deep understanding of both local practices and global trends. Businesses must carefully evaluate their IP portfolios to identify vulnerabilities, capitalize on opportunities, and ensure that their innovations are adequately safeguarded against unauthorized use, misappropriation, and infringement. This involves conducting thorough due diligence, implementing robust internal policies, and establishing effective mechanisms for detecting and responding to potential threats.

Furthermore, the rapid pace of technological advancement, particularly in fields such as artificial intelligence, biotechnology, and telecommunications, has introduced new challenges and complexities to IP law. Emerging issues such as the patentability of AI-generated inventions, the protection of data sets, and the enforcement of rights in the digital realm require innovative legal strategies and a forward-looking perspective. Our team is at the forefront of these developments, advising clients on how to adapt their IP strategies to mitigate risks and maximize value in an increasingly dynamic and interconnected global market.

In addition to traditional IP rights, we also recognize the importance of safeguarding trade secrets, confidential information, and know-how. These intangible assets often form the core competitive advantage of a business and require specific legal and practical measures to prevent unauthorized disclosure or misuse. We assist clients in drafting non-disclosure agreements (NDAs), establishing secure information management protocols, and pursuing legal action against former employees, competitors, or other parties who misappropriate sensitive commercial information.

The enforcement of IP rights in Turkey involves a multi-faceted approach that may include civil litigation, criminal prosecution, and customs seizures. Civil remedies typically involve seeking injunctions to halt infringing activities, claiming damages for financial losses, and requesting the confiscation and destruction of counterfeit goods. Criminal sanctions, which are particularly relevant in cases of trademark counterfeiting and copyright piracy, can result in significant fines and imprisonment for offenders. Customs measures play a crucial role in preventing the importation and exportation of infringing goods, providing an effective tool for disrupting illicit supply chains at the border.

Alternative dispute resolution (ADR) mechanisms, such as mediation and arbitration, are also becoming increasingly popular in the IP context. These methods offer a more confidential, efficient, and cost-effective alternative to traditional court proceedings, allowing parties to reach mutually beneficial settlements and preserve commercial relationships. Mandatory mediation for commercial disputes, including those involving IP rights, has been introduced in Turkey, emphasizing the importance of exploring amicable resolution strategies before resorting to litigation.
