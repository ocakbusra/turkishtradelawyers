Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy has been prepared by Turkish Trade Lawyers within the scope of Article 10 of the Law No. 6698 on Protection of Personal Data ("Law") and the Communiqué on the Procedures and Principles to be Followed in Fulfillment of the Obligation to Inform.

The purpose of this Cookie Policy is to inform you about the types of cookies we use, the purposes for which we use them, and how you can manage these cookies regarding the processing of personal data obtained automatically through the placement of cookies on your device on our website.

1. What is a Cookie?

A cookie is a small text file that is saved on your computer or mobile device via your browser when you visit a website. Cookies allow a website to work more efficiently, as well as to provide personalized pages to provide a faster and more suitable visit experience for your personal needs. Cookies only contain information about your visit history on the internet and do not collect any information about the files stored on your computer or mobile device.

2. How We Use Cookies?

We use cookies for various purposes such as:

  • To remember your preferences and store information about your use of our website.
  • To track how you use our website, such as where you click and which pages you visit.
  • To understand how our website is used and to improve your user experience.
  • To ensure the security and efficiency of our website.

3. Types of Cookies Used

According to their intended use, the following cookies are used on our website:

Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the proper functioning of our website. They are used to fulfill an information society service you have requested (such as logging in or filling out forms). These are usually session cookies and are deleted when you close your browser. Without these cookies, some parts of our website may not function correctly.

Performance and Analytics Cookies

These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. They help us to know which pages are the most and least popular and see how visitors move around the site. We use third-party services like Google Analytics for this purpose. The data collected is aggregated and therefore anonymous.

4. Legal Grounds and Purposes

Strictly necessary cookies are processed based on the legal ground that "processing of data is necessary for the legitimate interests of the data controller, provided that this usage does not violate the fundamental rights and freedoms of the data subject" (Article 5/2-f of the Law) or "it is necessary for the performance of a contract" (Article 5/2-c of the Law).

Performance and analytics cookies are processed based on your explicit consent where applicable, or our legitimate interest in understanding website usage to improve our services.

5. Your Rights

Data subjects whose personal data is processed through cookies can submit their requests regarding their rights within the scope of Article 11 of the Law via the e-mail address hi@turkishtradelawyers.org in accordance with the "Communiqué on the Procedures and Principles of Applications to the Data Controller".
