Overtime Pay

"How is overtime compensation calculated in Turkey?"

Quick Answer

Overtime exceeding 45 hours weekly is compensated at 1.5x normal rate. Maximum overtime is 270 hours annually. Employees can opt for time off instead.

Understanding Overtime Pay in Turkish Labor Law

In Turkish Labor Law (No. 4857), overtime is strictly regulated to protect employee health and balance employer needs. The standard weekly working time is set at a maximum of 45 hours. Any work performed beyond this statutory limit is legally classified as "overtime" (Fazla Çalışma) and is subject to specific wage multipliers or free time compensation.

Unlike the older Labor Law No. 1475 which focused on daily limits, the current Law No. 4857 primarily uses the weekly 45-hour limit as the main threshold for calculating overtime, while maintaining a strict daily cap to protect the worker's physical and mental well-being.

Two Distinct Concepts: Overtime vs. Extra Hours

Turkish law creates a unique distinction between two types of extended work depending on the employment contract:

  • Overtime (Fazla Çalışma): Work that exceeds the statutory limit of 45 hours per week. This is compensated by increasing the normal hourly wage by 50% (1.5x).
  • Working with Extra Hours (Fazla Sürelerle Çalışma): If the employment contract establishes a normal workweek of less than 45 hours (e.g., 40 hours), work performed between the contracted hours and the 45-hour statutory limit falls into this category. This is compensated by increasing the normal hourly wage by 25% (1.25x).

Statutory Limits on Overtime

Overtime is not unlimited. Employers must adhere to the following strict boundaries:

  • Daily Maximum: An employee's total working time, including overtime, cannot exceed 11 hours per day. Any work beyond 11 hours is considered overtime, regardless of whether the weekly 45-hour limit has been reached.
  • Annual Maximum: The total overtime worked by an employee cannot exceed 270 hours in one calendar year. This limit is designed to protect the employee's health. However, if an employee works beyond this 270-hour limit, the employer is still legally obligated to pay for those extra hours at the overtime rate.

Employee Consent and Right of Refusal

Except in cases of emergencies or mandatory state requirements, an employer cannot force an employee to work overtime. Written consent from the employee is mandatory. Previously, this consent had to be renewed annually, but recent regulatory changes allow it to be obtained once (e.g., during hiring). However, an employee who has previously consented to overtime has the statutory right to withdraw their consent by giving the employer 30 days' written notice.

Furthermore, even if consent is given, if the workplace physician determines that overtime poses a risk to the employee's health, the employee cannot be forced to work overtime.

Compliance Risk Indicators for Employers

  • Unpaid Overtime as Just Cause for Resignation: If an employer fails to pay overtime wages accurately and on time, the employee has the right to immediately terminate the contract for "just cause" (haklı neden) and demand severance pay.
  • Overtime Included in Salary Clauses: Clauses stating "overtime is included in the base salary" are valid only up to the 270-hour annual limit. Any overtime exceeding 270 hours per year must be compensated separately, regardless of the contract terms.
  • Equal Treatment Principle: While employers can choose who works overtime, consistently excluding certain employees (e.g., unionized workers) from profitable overtime without justification can violate the principle of equal treatment.

Alternative Compensation: Free Time (Serbest Zaman)

Instead of receiving increased wages, an employee who performs overtime has the legal right to request "free time" (paid leave) as compensation. For every hour of standard Overtime, the employee is entitled to 1.5 hours of free time. For every hour of Working with Extra Hours, they receive 1 hour and 15 minutes. This free time must be granted by the employer within six months of the request, without any deduction from regular wages.

Types of Overtime

Turkish Labor Law recognizes three distinct categories of overtime:

  1. Normal Overtime: Performed for general economic benefit, the nature of the work, or to increase production. Requires employee consent.
  2. Mandatory Overtime (Zorunlu Nedenlerle): Performed during breakdowns, to prevent imminent breakdowns, or due to force majeure (e.g., natural disasters affecting the workplace). Employee consent is not required, but it must only last as long as necessary to restore normal operations.
  3. Extraordinary Overtime: Mandated by Presidential decree during times of national mobilization or defense. Employee consent is not required.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

If my contract says overtime is included in my salary, can I still claim overtime pay?

Yes, partially. Under Supreme Court (Yargıtay) precedents, if you earn a high salary and signed a contract stating overtime is included, this is only valid for up to 270 hours per year. If you can prove you worked more than 270 hours in a year, you are legally entitled to separate compensation for those excess hours.

What happens if the employer exceeds the 11-hour daily limit?

The 11-hour daily limit is absolute. If an employee works more than 11 hours in a single day, the time exceeding 11 hours is automatically considered overtime and must be compensated at the 1.5x rate, even if the total weekly hours do not exceed 45 hours.

Can I be fired for refusing to work overtime?

If you have not given written consent, or if you formally withdrew your consent with 30 days' notice, refusing to work overtime is your legal right and cannot be used as a valid reason for dismissal. However, if you gave consent and refuse without a valid reason (e.g., a medical emergency), it may be considered a breach of your contractual duties.

How is overtime proven in court?

The burden of proof primarily lies with the employee. Overtime can be proven through written records (timesheets, entry/exit logs, internal emails) or, if documents are unavailable, through witness testimony. Witnesses must ideally be co-workers who worked the same hours.
