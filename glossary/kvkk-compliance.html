KVKK Compliance

"What are the key compliance requirements under Turkish data protection law (KVKK)?"

Quick Answer

Foreign companies processing the personal data of individuals in Türkiye are exposed to Turkish data protection penalties. Key requirements include appointing a Data Controller Representative, registering with VERBİS, and maintaining a localized, Türkiye-specific compliance framework.

Are You Exposed to Turkish Data Protection Penalties?

For foreign controllers processing personal data of Turkish residents, the KVKK regime is triggered from day one—there is no minimum threshold. Compliance involves critical steps to mitigate significant administrative fines under the 2026 guidelines.

1. Jurisdiction & Representative Obligation

Foreign Controllers: As a foreign data controller, the appointment of a Data Controller Representative and VERBİS registration obligation applies regardless of company size, turnover, or headcount. Even a Turkish branch or liaison office of a foreign entity remains subject to the Representative obligation under Turkish jurisprudence.

Local Entities: A Turkish-registered legal entity is not subject to the foreign Representative regime, but VERBİS registration thresholds may still apply.

2. Appointing a Data Controller Representative

This is the headline risk for foreign companies. Failure to address this can lead to severe penalties:

  • No Representative Appointed: Up to 12.5M TRY administrative fine for failure to appoint, plus up to 12.5M TRY for failure to register with VERBİS.
  • Appointed but Not Registered: Representative appointed but VERBİS registration outstanding carries up to 12.5M TRY exposure for the registration gap alone.
  • Full Compliance: A Representative appointed and registered with VERBİS satisfies the foundational obligation.

3. Documented Security Framework & GDPR Divergence

Relying solely on GDPR documentation is not sufficient, as Turkish jurisprudence has materially diverged. Localisation of your framework is strictly required.

The Risk: Having no Türkiye-specific data processing inventory or security framework exposes you to up to 15M TRY in fines for breach of data security obligations.

Key Risk Indicators

  • Monetary Fines: Failure to appoint a Representative, register with VERBİS, and localize documentation can result in cumulative fines exceeding 40M TRY under 2026 limits.
  • Beyond Fines: The KVKK Board may order the suspension of data processing, effectively halting your services in Türkiye, and may publicly publish its decision.
  • Continuous Monitoring: A current, Türkiye-specific framework materially reduces enforcement and reputational exposure.
