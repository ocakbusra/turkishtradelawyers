Data Controller Representative: KVKK Obligations for Foreign Companies

A 2026 Guide to Compliance for Non-Resident Entities

January 18, 2026 6 min read

The Turkish Personal Data Protection Law No. 6698 ("KVKK"), which came into full force in 2016, has significantly transformed the regulatory landscape for data privacy in Türkiye. Although the KVKK does not expressly regulate extraterritorial application in the same manner as the GDPR, the Turkish Personal Data Protection Board has consistently interpreted the Law to apply where personal data processing activities are directed at data subjects located in Türkiye or target the Turkish market.

As experienced Turkish lawyers, we frequently observe that foreign companies often overlook their local compliance requirements until they face substantial administrative sanctions. Central to these requirements for non-resident entities is the mandatory appointment of a Data Controller Representative.

1. The Extraterritorial Scope of the KVKK

The KVKK applies to data processing activities that are directed at individuals located in Türkiye or involve offering goods or services to the Turkish market, as interpreted through the established practice of the Turkish Personal Data Protection Board. Whether you are a global e-commerce platform, a SaaS provider, or a multinational corporation with no physical presence in the country, if you process the personal data of Turkish residents, you are bound by the KVKK.

2. The Mandate: Data Controller Representative

In accordance with Article 16 of the KVKK and the Regulation on the Data Controllers Registry, data controllers not established in Türkiye must appoint a "Data Controller Representative" (Veri Sorumlusu Temsilcisi). This obligation was further detailed by the Turkish Personal Data Protection Board's ("Board") Decision No. 2019/387.

The representative acts as the primary point of communication between the foreign data controller and the Turkish Personal Data Protection Authority ("Authority").

3. Who Must Appoint a Representative?

All natural or legal persons who:

  • Are resident or established outside of Türkiye, AND
  • Process personal data of data subjects in Türkiye.

Unlike local Turkish companies, foreign companies do not benefit from the "employee count" or "annual financial balance" exemptions (e.g., the 50 employees / 25 million TL threshold) that exempt some small local businesses from VERBİS registration.

Crucial Compliance Note: For foreign data controllers, registration with VERBİS and the appointment of a representative is a mandatory requirement regardless of their size, turnover, or employee count.

4. Qualifications and Duties of the Representative

The representative must be either:

  • A Turkish citizen residing in Türkiye, or
  • A legal entity established in Türkiye.

The Board Decision No. 2019/387 clarifies that the representative's duties include:

  • Communication: Facilitating correspondence between the Authority and the data controller.
  • Data Subject Requests: Receiving and facilitating responses to applications made by data subjects in Türkiye.
  • VERBİS Management: Executing and updating the registration on the Data Controllers Registry Information System (VERBİS).

It is important to note that the appointment of a representative does not shift the legal liability from the foreign data controller. The foreign entity remains the "Data Controller" and is ultimately responsible for compliance.

5. VERBİS Registration: A Legal Prerequisite

Registration with VERBİS is not merely a formality; it is a public declaration of your data processing landscape. Foreign companies must disclose:

  • Categories of data processed.
  • Purposes of processing.
  • Recipient groups.
  • Data retention periods.
  • Technical and administrative security measures.
  • Cross-border data transfers.

The representative performs this registration on behalf of the foreign controller using a secure electronic signature.

6. 2026 Administrative Sanctions and Enforcement Risks

Non-compliance with the KVKK, particularly the failure to register with VERBİS or appoint a representative, carries severe financial and reputational risks. Under Article 18 of the Law, the Board is authorized to impose administrative fines, which are adjusted annually according to the revaluation rate.

As of 2026, administrative fines imposed under Article 18 of the KVKK are updated annually in accordance with the revaluation rate announced pursuant to the Turkish Tax Procedure Law. Accordingly, violations such as failure to register with VERBİS or failure to appoint a Data Controller Representative may result in significant administrative fines, depending on the nature and severity of the breach.

Furthermore, the Board has the authority to order the cessation of data processing activities or the deletion of unlawfully processed data, which can lead to significant operational disruptions for global businesses.

In addition to monetary sanctions, the Board may impose corrective measures including suspension of data processing activities, restriction of cross-border data transfers, and mandatory deletion, destruction, or anonymization of unlawfully processed personal data. These measures may cause substantial operational disruption for foreign companies operating without proper KVKK compliance.

7. Conclusion

For foreign companies, the Turkish market offers vast opportunities, but these come with a rigorous data protection compliance burden. Appointing a Data Controller Representative is the first and most vital step toward ensuring KVKK compliance.

As the Authority increases its oversight and audit activities, non-resident companies must act proactively. Legal counsel specializing in Turkish data protection law is essential to navigate the nuances of VERBİS registration and to ensure that the relationship with the local representative is structured to mitigate risk effectively.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Who must appoint a Data Controller Representative in Turkey?

Any data controller residing or established outside of Turkey who processes personal data of individuals residing in Turkey must appoint a representative.

Are there exemptions for small foreign companies?

No. Unlike local companies, foreign data controllers must register with VERBİS and appoint a representative regardless of their employee count or annual turnover.

What are the duties of a Data Controller Representative?

Duties include facilitating communication with the Authority, responding to data subject requests, and managing VERBİS registration.

What is VERBİS registration?

VERBİS is a public registry system where data controllers declare their data processing activities, including data categories, purposes, and security measures.

What are the penalties for non-compliance?

Administrative fines for failure to register with VERBİS are severe, ranging significantly high based on annual revaluation rates, potentially exceeding 9 million TRY.
