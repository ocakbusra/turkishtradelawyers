Navigating Local Procedural Safeguards

01

The Security Deposit Exemption (Cautio Judicatum Solvi)

Under Turkish civil procedure, foreign plaintiffs launching lawsuits must generally post a substantial financial security deposit to cover potential court costs. For Irish companies, a vital exception applies. Under the 1954 Hague Convention on Civil Procedure, a strict principle of mutual reciprocity is established. Irish corporations and citizens are exempt from posting this security deposit in Turkish commercial courts, allowing you to launch enforcement or debt collection actions without tying up capital.

02

Resolving the Power of Attorney (POA) Delay

We manage your local litigation, contract signings, or subsidiary affairs completely remotely without requiring your corporate officers to travel. You can execute a broad-authority corporate POA through primary method: before an Irish notary public, followed by an Irish Department of Foreign Affairs (DFA) Hague Apostille. To verify signing authority, a recent certified Companies Registration Office (CRO) printout must be appended to the document to verify corporate signing authority.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common legal inquiries from German corporate clients operating in Türkiye.

1. We are a German company. How can we issue a Power of Attorney to your law firm?

You can execute a Power of Attorney before a German Notary Public (Notar). It must then receive a Landgericht Apostille. Alternatively, your directors can visit the Turkish Consulates in Frankfurt, Munich, or Berlin to issue the document directly in Turkish, bypassing translation delays.

2. Are German court judgments directly enforceable in Türkiye?

No. Türkiye is not an EU member state. A German judgment requires a formal recognition and enforcement lawsuit (Tanınma ve Tenfiz) in a Turkish court under international private law principles before local assets can be seized.

3. How long does it take to establish a German subsidiary in Istanbul?

Typically 5 to 7 business days once we receive your original, apostilled Handelsregisterauszug and board resolutions in Türkiye.

4. Can our Turkish software development team be governed by German employment law?

No. Turkish employment law contains strict mandatory provisions protecting employees physically working in Türkiye. You must use localized Turkish employment contracts to avoid severe penalties and labor lawsuits.

5. Are German companies required to pay court security deposits in Türkiye?

No. Thanks to the Sino-German-Turkish Convention and strict reciprocity principles, German corporations are exempt from posting the mandatory cash security deposit (Cautio Judicatum Solvi) normally required from foreign plaintiffs.

6. A Turkish supplier has failed to deliver our order. Can we freeze their assets?

Yes. Through a preliminary attachment (İhtiyati Haciz), we can often secure court orders to freeze bank accounts and real estate within days if there is strong, documented evidence of the debt and risk of evasion.

7. Does a Turkish subsidiary need a local Turkish director?

No. A Turkish Joint Stock Company (A.Ş.) or Limited Liability Company (Ltd. Şti.) can be 100% owned by your German parent company (GmbH or AG), and German citizens can serve as sole directors.

8. Are German data protection frameworks (GDPR) sufficient in Türkiye?

While similar, Türkiye operates under its own data protection law (KVKK). You cannot rely solely on GDPR compliance. You must fulfill local obligations, including mandatory registration with the Turkish data controllers' registry (VERBIS) and executing cross-border data transfer agreements.

9. How do we verify a Turkish business before sending a large payment?

We conduct rapid due diligence, checking the official Trade Registry (MERSİS), litigation records, bankruptcy status, and performing physical site visits to verify the company’s legitimacy and protect your capital from phoenix entities.

10. Can we terminate a Turkish distributor if they underperform?

Yes, but it must be handled carefully. Under Turkish law, a terminated exclusive distributor might claim a portfolio indemnity (goodwill compensation). We structure termination notices preemptively to mitigate these claims.

11. Are there tax treaties between Germany and Türkiye?

Yes, Germany and Türkiye have a Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement. When structured correctly, this treaty optimizes the tax burden on dividend repatriations, royalties, and corporate service fees between the two jurisdictions.
