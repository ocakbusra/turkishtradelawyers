Drafting a Valid Power of Attorney for Use in Turkey: Apostille, Notary, and Consular Certification Explained

January 28, 2026 8 min read

Granting a power of attorney (PoA) for use in Turkey is a critical procedural step for foreign individuals and companies conducting legal, commercial, or administrative transactions without being physically present in the country. However, incorrectly drafted or improperly certified powers of attorney frequently lead to rejection by Turkish notaries, courts, land registries, or banks.

This article provides a practical and legally accurate guide on how to prepare a valid power of attorney for use in Turkey, focusing on apostille requirements, notary formalities, and consular certification pathways.

1. Why Powers of Attorney Are Heavily Scrutinized in Turkey

Under Turkish practice, a power of attorney is not treated as a mere authorization letter. It is a strict formal legal instrument.

Common transactions requiring a valid PoA include:

  • Company incorporation and shareholder resolutions
  • Court representation and litigation
  • Real estate purchase or sale
  • Banking and account opening
  • Customs and tax procedures
  • M&A and share transfers

Turkish authorities verify both form and substance. Even minor defects may render the PoA unusable.

2. Legal Framework Governing Powers of Attorney in Turkey

A power of attorney used in Turkey is primarily assessed under:

  • Turkish Code of Obligations (representation rules)
  • Turkish Notary Law (form and certification)
  • International private law principles
  • Apostille Convention (Hague Convention of 1961)

The key principle is formal equivalence: A foreign-issued PoA must meet the same formal reliability standards as a Turkish notarial power of attorney.

3. Two Valid Ways to Issue a Power of Attorney for Turkey

3.1 Issuing a Power of Attorney Abroad with Apostille

This is the most common route.

Step-by-step process:

  • Draft the power of attorney text
  • Sign before a local notary public
  • Obtain an apostille from the competent authority
  • Translate into Turkish by a sworn translator in Turkey
  • Notarize the Turkish translation

Once completed, the document can be used before Turkish authorities.

Key condition: The country of issuance must be a party to the Hague Apostille Convention.

3.2 Issuing a Power of Attorney via Turkish Consulates

If the issuing country is not an apostille country, or if maximum certainty is required, the power of attorney may be issued directly before a Turkish consulate.

Advantages:

  • No apostille required
  • Direct acceptance by Turkish notaries and courts
  • Turkish language version prepared at source

This method is frequently preferred for high-value transactions or litigation mandates.

4. Apostille in Turkey: What It Does and What It Does Not Do

An apostille:

  • Certifies the authenticity of the notary’s signature
  • Confirms the official capacity of the signing authority

An apostille does not:

  • Validate the content of the power of attorney
  • Cure drafting defects
  • Replace Turkish translation requirements

Incorrect scope or missing powers remain invalid even with an apostille.

5. Mandatory Content Requirements Under Turkish Practice

A power of attorney intended for Turkey must be explicit.

5.1 General vs. Special Authority

Certain acts require special and express authorization, such as:

  • Selling or purchasing real estate
  • Representing before courts
  • Waiving rights
  • Signing settlement agreements
  • Share transfers

Generic language is insufficient. Turkish notaries regularly reject vague mandates.

5.2 Identity and Capacity Details

The PoA must clearly include:

  • Full name (exact passport spelling)
  • Nationality
  • Passport number
  • Residential address
  • Legal capacity statement

For companies:

  • Full corporate name
  • Registration number
  • Authorized signatory details
  • Corporate authorization reference

6. Language and Translation Rules

Turkish authorities operate exclusively in Turkish.

Therefore:

  • A sworn Turkish translation is mandatory
  • Translation must be notarized in Turkey
  • The translator’s certification is examined

Translations prepared abroad are commonly rejected unless re-certified in Turkey.

7. Common Mistakes That Invalidate Powers of Attorney

The most frequent issues include:

  • Missing apostille
  • Apostille placed on translation instead of original
  • Insufficient scope of authority
  • Incorrect personal details
  • Use of outdated passport numbers
  • Corporate powers without board authorization

These mistakes often surface after transactions are initiated, causing delays and legal risk.

8. Practical Checklist Before Using a Power of Attorney in Turkey

Before submitting a PoA, verify:

  • Issuance country is apostille-compliant
  • Apostille is correctly attached to the original
  • Content includes explicit authority
  • Turkish sworn translation is notarized
  • Corporate signatories are properly authorized

A pre-use legal review significantly reduces rejection risk.

9. Why Turkish Notaries and Courts Apply Strict Standards

Turkey operates under a preventive notarial system. Notaries are legally liable for accepting defective documents.

As a result:

  • They apply conservative interpretation
  • They require clear, express wording
  • They prioritize form over intent

This approach explains why “valid abroad” does not automatically mean “usable in Turkey”.

10. Conclusion

A power of attorney intended for use in Turkey must be drafted with precision, certified correctly, and aligned with Turkish formal requirements. Apostille compliance alone is insufficient without proper content and translation.

For foreign individuals and companies, a structured and legally informed approach to issuing powers of attorney prevents costly delays and operational setbacks.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Do I need an apostille for a power of attorney used in Turkey?

Yes, unless the power of attorney is issued directly before a Turkish consulate.

Can I use a power of attorney drafted in English?

Yes, but it must be translated into Turkish by a sworn translator and notarized in Turkey.

Is a general power of attorney sufficient for court representation?

No. Court representation requires explicit and special authorization.

Can a company issue a power of attorney without a board resolution?

In most cases, no. Turkish practice expects proof of signatory authority.

How long does it take to prepare a valid power of attorney?

Typically 3 to 7 business days, depending on apostille and translation timing.

Will Turkish authorities accept electronic or scanned powers of attorney?

No. Original notarized documents are required.
