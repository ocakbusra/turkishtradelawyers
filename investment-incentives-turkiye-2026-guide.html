Investment Incentives & Exemptions: A 2026 Guide to State Support for Foreign Capital in Türkiye

Türkiye continues to position itself as a highly attractive hub for global capital, driven by its strategic location, evolving infrastructure, and comprehensive state support mechanisms. Under the Foreign Direct Investment Law No. 4875, Türkiye strictly adheres to the principle of "national treatment," ensuring absolute equality between domestic and foreign investors. Consequently, 100% foreign-owned entities that meet the necessary criteria and obtain an Investment Incentive Certificate from the Ministry of Industry and Technology are entitled to the exact same cost-reducing state supports as local firms.

Here is an analytical breakdown of the current investment incentive system, high-tech specific programs, and direct tax exemptions available to foreign direct investments as of 2026.

1. Core Exemptions and Supports Under the Investment Incentive Certificate

While the exact benefits vary depending on the investment's location (provinces are divided into 6 socio-economic development regions), scale, and sector, a standard Investment Incentive Certificate provides the following primary financial advantages:

  • Value Added Tax (VAT) Exemption: No VAT is paid on machinery and equipment procured domestically or internationally within the scope of the certificate, significantly reducing initial capital expenditures.
  • Customs Duty Exemption: Machinery and equipment imported from abroad for the investment are entirely exempt from customs duties.
  • Corporate Tax Reduction: Corporate Tax calculated on commercial profits is reduced by up to 80% (and up to 100% for project-based investments) until the total deducted tax reaches the government-approved "Contribution to Investment" threshold.
  • Social Security Premium (Employer's Share) Support: For any additional employment created by the new investment, the employer's share of the social security premium (calculated based on the minimum wage) is covered by the state. This support lasts for 2 years in highly developed regions and can extend up to 10 years in the 6th Region (Eastern and Southeastern Anatolia).
  • Interest or Dividend Support: For investments financed through commercial loans with a maturity of at least one year, a certain portion of the interest or dividend payments is reimbursed by the state (capped at 70% of the fixed investment amount).
  • Investment Land Allocation: State-owned land may be allocated for large-scale investments benefiting from strategic, priority, or regional support frameworks.

2. Prominent Strategic Investment Programs in 2026

Türkiye's current incentive architecture heavily prioritizes value-added manufacturing and technological transformation. The three main mechanisms offering the highest potential for foreign investors are:

A. The HIT-30 High Technology Investment Program

Announced as a $30 billion "Super Incentive" program, HIT-30 aims to turn the country into a global technology base. It opens doors for investors in over 30 critical fields, including semiconductors, mobility, green energy (hydrogen, wind, batteries), artificial intelligence, quantum computing, and industrial robotics.

  • Minimum Requirement: Varies by call, but generally requires a minimum fixed investment of 2 billion TRY (or 1 billion TRY including R&D).
  • Exclusive Supports: Offers tax incentives up to 60%, direct grant support covering 30-40% of the investment amount, advantageous financing up to 70%, and energy subsidies covering up to 10% of energy costs. For instance, AI or quantum hardware investments receive an additional 20% grant support.

B. Priority Investments System

Regardless of regional development disparities, certain investments strategically supported by Türkiye automatically benefit from 5th Region incentives (the second most advantageous tier).

Data centers, renewable energy (solar/wind) manufacturing, defense industry, pharmaceutical production, and agricultural greenhouse investments fall under this highly lucrative scope.

C. Project-Based Incentive System

For massive investments with exceptionally high economic volume, employment capacity, and the potential to reduce import dependency, the President can decree tailor-made, "custom-fit" support packages that exceed standard statutory limits. These can include specialized wage support for highly qualified personnel and energy subsidies lasting up to 10 years.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) for Foreign Investors

Q1: Is a foreign company required to find a local partner to benefit from incentives in Türkiye?

Analytical Answer: No. Under Turkish law, you can establish a 100% foreign-owned Joint Stock Company (A.Ş.) or Limited Liability Company (Ltd. Şti.). Once established, this entity is treated as a legal person subject to the Turkish Commercial Code and can apply for the exact same incentive programs as any domestic firm.

Q2: Do foreign investors need to reside in Türkiye or obtain a residence permit to establish a company and get an incentive certificate?

Analytical Answer: No, physical residency is not mandatory. Foreign investors can own a company without living in Türkiye or having a work/residence permit. However, an authorized legal representative or manager must be appointed in Türkiye to handle official procedures (tax office, banking, and incentive applications via the government's E-TUYS system).

Q3: When do the VAT and Customs Duty exemptions under the Investment Incentive Certificate become active?

Analytical Answer: These exemptions become active the moment the incentive certificate is approved. Upon importation, presenting the certificate to the relevant customs administration applies the exemption instantly. Tax reductions, on the other hand, begin applying once the investment starts generating commercial profit.

Q4: Can a foreign company in the software and technology sector benefit from incentives even if it doesn't import physical machinery?

Analytical Answer: Absolutely. Particularly under the HIT-30 program and the Priority Investments framework, there are substantial grants and tax exemptions for software purchases (which are covered by VAT exemption), R&D expenditures, market development activities, data center infrastructure, and salaries for qualified IT personnel. The state's focus is on high-tech output, not just heavy industry.
